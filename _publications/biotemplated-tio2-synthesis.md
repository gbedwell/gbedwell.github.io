Virus-derived architectures provide robust templates for nanoparticle assembly. This paper uses the bacteriophage P22 with selective modifications to facilitate the biotemplated synthesis of photocatalytic titanium dioxide from a water-soluble precursor. Critically, the synthesized TiO2 is retained within the protein cage of the capsid lattice, effectively solubilizing the otherwise insoluble photocatalytic material. Additionally, we show that by driving TiO2 formation with distinct catalytic peptides we are able to synthesize both the rutile and anatase forms of TiO2. These results highlight the tunable nature of our nanoparticle-based system.
