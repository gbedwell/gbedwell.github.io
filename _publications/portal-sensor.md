dsDNA bacteriophage and herpes viruses utilize ATP-dependent packaging motors to package their genome into the viral capsid. Genome packaging occurs after capsid assembly through the portal vertex, a ring-like oligomeric complex that exists at one of the twelve pentameric vertices in the icosahedral capsid lattice. Rather than terminating this packaging process in a sequence-dependent manner, these viruses utilize another mechanism to "sense" the amount of packaged DNA and terminate the process when appropriate. This paper reveals that portal proteins serve as the molecular sensor in this process. Targeted mutations in the interior of the P22 portal protein alter the inherent dynamics and compressibility of the portal protein and thereby change the amount of DNA packaged into viruses.
