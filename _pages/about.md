I am a post-doctoral researcher in the [Engelman Laboratory](https://engelmanlab.med.harvard.edu/) at the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute with a secondary appointment at Harvard Medical School. 

I received my B.S. in Chemistry and my Ph.D. in Biophysics under the tutelage of Peter Prevelige from the University of Alabama at Birmingham. During my Ph.D. I studied various aspects of virus and nanoparticle assembly using an assortment of biophysical techniques including analytical ultracentrifugation, static and dynamic light scattering, structural mass spectrometry, and electron micoscopy. The data analysis routines common to these techniques sparked my interest in computational modeling and data analysis in general. Since then, I have sought to combine my wet bench skillset with my burgeoning computational skillset in order to address biological problems in novel and powerful ways.

In the Engelman lab, my research is focused primarily on better defining the nuclear landscape of HIV-1 integration and the factors that influence its formation. In the future, I hope to apply the knowledge gleaned from this work to developing novel therapeutic strategies aimed at curing HIV-1 infection.

Beyond scientific interests
------
Outside of the laboratory, I happily spend the majority of my time with my wife and daughter. We enjoy fantastical stories, good food, and music we can sing along to. I've been known to obsess from time to time over various topics or events (my family thoughtfully tolerates my pedantry), and my weekend mornings are usually spent watching an assortment of football matches on television. I enjoy listening to and discovering good music and I am always happy to read a good book.
